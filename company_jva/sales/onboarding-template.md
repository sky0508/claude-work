# JVA IB — Company Onboarding Guide

> Version: 1.0 | Created: 2026-03-13
> Use this document when onboarding a new company to the IB platform.

---

## Onboarding Flow Overview

```
Company shows interest (inbound or outbound)
        ↓
Schedule a meeting
        ↓
Introduce IB platform at the meeting
        ↓
Company agrees to list a position
        ↓
Send Job Card Google Form to company contact
        ↓
Company fills out the form
        ↓
IB creates the Job Card and distributes to LINE / platform
```

---

## Step 1: Meeting & Agreement

At the meeting, cover the following:

- [ ] Introduce what IB is and how it works (use `output/jva-ib-guide.md` as reference)
- [ ] Explain the student profile (English-focused, top universities, beginner Japanese)
- [ ] Confirm position requirements are realistic (see recommended requirements below)
- [ ] Share Platform Rules — especially the **4-business-day response SLA**
- [ ] Get verbal agreement to list the position

---

## Step 2: Send Job Card Form

After agreement, send the Job Card Google Form to the company contact.

The form collects the following:

| Field | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Company name | |
| Contact person & email | |
| Position title | |
| English requirement | Business / Native / Not required |
| Japanese requirement | Not required / N3+ / Business |
| Weekly hours | Recommend 15–25h |
| Compensation | ¥/h or monthly |
| Location | Tokyo in-person / Remote / Hybrid |
| Work authorization required | Yes / No |
| Key responsibilities | 3–5 bullet points |
| Ideal candidate profile | 2–3 sentences |

> **Recommended requirements for high match rate:**
> - Weekly hours: 15–25h (not 35–40h)
> - Japanese: N3 or lower preferred, or not required
> - Location: Remote-friendly or flexible
> - Experience: Entry-level to intermediate

## Step 4: Platform Rules（掲載条件）

The following rules apply to all companies listed on IB.
Communicate these clearly at onboarding.

### 3-1. Response SLA

- **Reply to applicants within 4 business days** of receiving their CV.
- IB will send a reminder if no response is received after 4 business days.
- If a company repeatedly fails to respond, their listing will be **temporarily suspended**.

### 3-2. Selection Process

- Companies must notify IB of the outcome for each applicant (rejected / interview / offer).
- Selection progress should be updated within 2 weeks of CV receipt.

### 3-3. Listing Suspension Policy

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| No reply after 4 business days | IB sends reminder |
| No reply after 2nd reminder (within 2 weeks) | Listing temporarily suspended |
| Repeated non-response across multiple applicants | Listing removed until further notice |

---

## Step 5: LINE Distribution

Once Job Card is confirmed:

- [ ] Add position to LINE message queue
- [ ] Schedule distribution in next weekly LINE broadcast
- [ ] Confirm company contact receives applications via automated email (GAS)

---

## Step 6: Follow-up Cadence

After listing goes live:

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Day 1–4 after application | Wait for company response |
| Day 5 (no response) | Send reminder to company |
| Day 14 | Check selection status — update IB records |
| Monthly | Review if position is still active |

- We'll check in periodically to track selection progress.

Looking forward to finding you a great intern!

Best,
[Your name]
JVA IB Team
