## Discussion Notes

### 1. Sponsorship Plan Review

**Pricing & tiers**
- Kuei: current pricing (Bronze/Silver/Gold) is reasonable for now, but should be treated as a live document — adjust frequently based on market feedback.
- Recommended approach: conduct **hearing meetings** first (no pitching, no price talk), just gather feedback on how companies feel about sponsoring, what they'd value, what events appeal to them → use that to refine pricing.

**Per-event vs. organization-wide sponsorship**
- Kuei: companies are unlikely to sponsor JBA as a whole for a year — too much reputational risk if a specific event goes poorly.
- **Decision: sponsorship should be framed per-event, not org-wide.**
- Exception: long-term/title sponsorships are possible where the event is recurring and predictable (e.g., Bootcamp with Shibuya Startup, or IB platform sponsorship).
- Current tiers in the plan are drafted as org-wide → **next step: revise to per-event framing.**
- IB platform sponsorship (companies paying for access to students) → to be kept under IB's scope, not Partnership.

**Hearing meeting approach** (Kuei's experience)
- First meeting: don't mention price. Focus on relationship and getting them to know JBA. Subtly mention the sponsorship plan at the end if it feels natural.
- Second meeting: more direct pitch.
- Best channel: attend events, network in person, then reach out. Cold outreach has the lowest success rate but is the easiest entry point.
- Key insight from Take Up Tokyo: got Deloitte sponsorship by attending 4+ events and building a personal connection first.
- Kuei encouraged the team to actively research and learn new sponsorship acquisition strategies (books, YouTube, professionals).

**Value we offer sponsors**
- The biggest asset is access to our people (event participants).
- Idea: with proper consent/disclaimer at events, we can share participant emails with sponsors who are targeting talent.
- Privacy and legal considerations need to be addressed in event registration.

### 2. Trial Member Task Design

**Nahi's proposal**
- Trial members should **not do real outreach** before April 2nd.
- Rationale: if a trial member who doesn't get accepted manages to close a sponsor, it creates an awkward situation.
- Until April 2nd (end of trial period), tasks are **preparation-only**:
  - Draft cold outreach message templates
  - Build outreach tracking system
  - Create an explanation slide (JBA overview for pitching)
  - Create a meeting checklist / guide for hearing meetings

**Real outreach timeline**
- Target start date for actual outreach: **April 11–12 (second week of April)**, after trial members become core members and prep is complete.

**Sponsorship proposal task**
- Kuei: trial members should be tasked with creating a **customized sponsorship proposal for a specific target company** (not just a template).
- A real proposal is a better evaluation task — it can't be Googled or ChatGPT-ed; it requires knowing JBA and the company.
- Nahi to create a proposal template first → trial members use that as a guide to produce a real proposal for one specific company.
- Format: document (not slide).

**Target company database**
- Trial task: fill in the company database.
- Nahi to simplify the ideal company profile criteria to **3–5 key characteristics** (current list is too long).
- Kuei to seed the list with ~10 target companies + reasoning, so the research direction is strategic, not random.
